Why it's different

An instrument, not a library.

Built for doing, not reading. Six things separate it from every strategy deck and framework collection you've already been disappointed by.

01

Built for doing

One spine and a router that sends you to the one framework the problem needs — not all seven. The app is the instrument played, not described.

02

Communication gets equal weight

Most strategy products stop at the decision. This one carries it to behaviour — one canonical artifact, kept true, that people can actually hold in their heads.

03

Frameworks stripped to working parts

Each lens reduced to its operative anatomy, each with a "failure smell" that tells you when you're doing it wrong. No jargon-as-moat.

04

Problem-first, not framework-first

Lenses, not answers. You start from the situation and reach for the right tool, instead of forcing every problem through a favourite model.

05

AI-native

The vault doubles as a context pack. Point your assistant at it and it challenges the work against the frameworks' own quality bars — not generic strategy soup.

06

A feedback spine

Track → reality disagrees → back to Understand. A method for continuous strategy work, where every broken bet makes the next strategy sharper.
